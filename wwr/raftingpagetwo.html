Rivers

This page provides information about the rivers we raft on, offering a unique glimpse into each river's beauty and excitement.

Main Fork of the Salmon River, Idaho

Nestled in the Frank Church Wilderness, the Salmon River—also known as the River of No Return—is a place where visitors fall in love with its rugged beauty and wildlife. The river features Class I-IV rapids and is one of the longest undammed rivers in the United States.

Desolation and Gray Canyon, Utah

Located in Utah, this 84-mile trip on the Green River offers stunning desert scenery, prehistoric sites, and Class II/III rapids, making for a scenic six-day journey.

Grand Canyon, Arizona

Experience world-class whitewater and breathtaking views in the Grand Canyon. Rapids are rated on a unique scale from I-X, and you’ll find ancient ruins and side canyons accessible only by river.

FAQ

Is this a good trip for kids?

Yes! Children over the age of 8 generally enjoy the trips more. The scenic beauty and rapids keep things interesting for kids and adults alike.

Is whitewater rafting safe for non-swimmers?

Safety is a priority, with certified guides and coast guard-approved life vests. Non-swimmers often have a great time, and a full safety briefing is provided before the trip.

What is included in the trip?

We provide all camping equipment, meals, drinks, and safety gear. Guests may bring limited amounts of personal drinks as well.

What should I bring?

Pack for changing weather with both cool and warm gear. Essentials include a rain jacket, quick-dry clothes, a hat, sunscreen, and a water bottle.

Why go whitewater rafting?

A day on the river is filled with rapids, calm sections, hikes, scenic views, great food, and stories under a starry sky—it's an experience you won't forget.

How far in advance should I book?

We recommend booking a year in advance for specific dates or six months if you’re flexible.
